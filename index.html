What LLM can my computer run?

The honest answer depends on two numbers: your GPU's VRAM and your system RAM. A model's weight file has to fit in VRAM, and its KV cache grows with the context length you want, so a 7B model at Q4 that fits at a short context can spill over at 32K tokens. Rigfit does that arithmetic for every model and quantization in its catalog and ranks the ones that fit your exact rig, so you stop guessing and downloading 15 GB of weights to find out.

How Rigfit sizes a model to your rig

  • Weights in VRAM. Each quantization (Q4_K_M, Q5_K_M, Q8_0, and so on) has a different file size. Rigfit checks whether the weights fit your VRAM first.
  • Context in the leftover. Whatever VRAM is left after the weights holds the KV cache. Rigfit computes the longest context you can actually reach, and marks a fit yellow when it has to truncate the model's full window.
  • RAM offload as a fallback. When the weights are a little too big for VRAM, part of the model can run from system RAM. Rigfit includes those fits and flags the offload, instead of silently dropping the model or pretending it runs at full speed.

Frequently asked questions

Can I run this LLM locally?
Enter your GPU VRAM and system RAM and Rigfit shows every model and quantization that fits, with a green (comfortable), yellow (tight), or red (won't fit) badge. If a model isn't in the ranked list, your current rig can't run it without heavy offload.
Is there a local LLM VRAM calculator?
Yes. Rigfit is a VRAM calculator that works in reverse. Instead of you plugging one model into a formula, it runs the weight and KV-cache math across a whole catalog and returns the models that fit, ranked by how much headroom they leave.
What's the best model for my GPU?
The best model is the largest, highest-quality quantization that still fits your VRAM at a usable context length. Rigfit sorts by fit quality first, then boosts models that are trending on Hugging Face this week, so the top of the list is a genuinely good pick right now rather than the biggest name.
How often is the trending list updated?
A scheduled script pulls trending text-generation models from Hugging Face and recent local-LLM discussion from Hacker News once a week, so a model that's picking up steam moves toward the top of your matched results.
